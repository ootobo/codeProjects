Ambassador Eloho E. Otobo

A true treasure from Delta

Here's a time line of Ambassador Otobo's life:

  • 1951 - Born in Otibio-Ohwe (Land of Coconut)
  • 1963 - Graduated from St. Peters Primary School in Otibio-Owhe
  • 1969 - Worked briefly at Niger River Transport Company as a Clerk
  • 1970 - Became Clerical Officer and posted to the State Government Treasury at Ughelli, Delta State
  • 1971 - Passed the Advanced level examinations at the first attempt, was admitted the following year to University of Lagos
  • 1975 - Graduated with a Bachelors of Science degree in Sociology, at the Second Class-Upper Level
  • 1976 - completed the National Youth Service Corps
  • 1978 - Married his wife Esther Aruoriwo Uroghome from Emevor
  • 1981 - Got posted to the Embassy of Nigeria in Beijing, People’s Republic of China
He was a prolific writer with a profound knowledge of all aspects of African development and has published widely on the subject including OpEds in the Guardian and a book “Africa in Transition: A New Way of Looking at Progress in the Region” was published in 2017. Africa has lost a true treasure.
-- Yvette Stevens
